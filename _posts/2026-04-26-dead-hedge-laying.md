Meeting on Eaves playground at 10am, we will continue to create a clearing in penny fields and build up the perimeter dead wood barrier to prevent deer from snacking on the native downy birch and oak. Most of the beech saplings in penny fields have been removed now, but the barrier needs some additional work to make it more sustainable, and needs building up with more beech saplings from the surrounding woodland. This builds on the work we carried out in our [first session of dead hedge laying on the 22nd March]({% post_url 2026-03-22-dead-hedge-laying %}).

## Report from the day

Great session today as we wrapped up our first regeneration area 🌱

We’re planning a second site further up the Clough, in the more temperate zone. It’ll be really interesting to compare the two over time as the understory develops without pressure from browsing and trampling 🦌 

You can already spot encouraging signs in the Penny Field regeneration area, with bramble, holly, and ferns beginning to establish and grow undisturbed by deer 🙌
