Meeting on Eaves playground at 10am, we will continue to create a clearing in penny fields and build up the perimeter dead wood barrier to prevent deer from snacking on the native downy birch and oak. Most of the beech saplings in penny fields have been removed now, but the barrier needs some additional work to make it more sustainable, and needs building up with more beech saplings from the surrounding woodland.

The area we have cleared was populated by a large number of Beech saplings and small trees, seeded by the large beech on the other side of the woodland trail which leads down to the river. Now it is clear, we are hoping that native flora lying dormant in the soil will take advantage of the increased sunlight and create an understorey, which will increase the biodiversity of the site.

## Report from the day

What a fun and productive day! 🌿

Today’s dead hedge making session really showed what a group of passionate people can achieve together 😊

We started by clearing some slightly larger beech saplings and using them as sturdy stakes, driving them in with a sledgehammer 🔨

Then we packed in the material we’d cleared during our last session, building up a lovely functional dead hedge!

It’s not only great habitat for wildlife, but should also help protect the regeneration area from deer (fingers crossed!)

As well as making great progress, it was a welcome opportunity to learn and share hedge laying skills, enjoy the outdoors, and get a solid bit of exercise in too!.

There’s something so satisfying about turning “waste” into something valuable for nature🌱

We’ll be continuing this work over the next few sessions, so if you’d like to get involved, we’d love to see you there!
