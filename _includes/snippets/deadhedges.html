One of the most valuable aspects of a dead hedge is its positive impact on biodiversity. It provides shelter for a variety of wildlife, such as hedgehogs and field mice who often seek refuge within the protective brushwood, while birds use it for nesting and roosting. Insects, including beetles, bees, and butterflies, thrive within the decomposing wood, contributing to a rich and diverse ecosystem. Over time, the decomposition of wood within the hedge enriches the surrounding soil. Fungi and microorganisms break down the organic matter, recycling nutrients and improving soil health and enhancing nearby plant growth.
